let's adapt the models to a credit card fraud detection scenario. This is a common and relevant problem in machine learning, and it will 
allow us to demonstrate some important techniques for imbalanced datasets.

Problem Statement:

Business Problem: Credit card fraud results in significant financial losses for businesses and individuals.
Importance: Detecting fraudulent transactions is crucial to minimize these losses, protect customers, and maintain the integrity of financial systems.
Machine Learning Task: We can formulate this as a binary classification problem, where the goal is to classify each transaction as either "fraudulent"
or "not fraudulent."
                                                                    
Dataset:

I am using a credit card fraud dataset. A popular one is the "Credit Card Fraud Detection" dataset available on Kaggle.
                                                                           
Here the link of the Dataset :- https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/mlg-ulb/creditcardfraud
                                                                           
Key Characteristics of Credit Card Fraud Datasets:
Imbalanced: Typically, the number of non-fraudulent transactions is much higher than fraudulent transactions.
High dimensionality: Datasets often contain many features (e.g., transaction amount, time, location, user information).
Anonymized: Features are often transformed or anonymized for privacy reasons.
